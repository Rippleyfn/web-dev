Meditations on First Philosophy

A philosophical treatise by René Descartes first published in Latin in 1641. It is a foundational work of modern Western philosophy that uses a "method of doubt" to establish a secure, rational foundation for knowledge.

The 48 Laws of Power

Made by Robert Greene, it is a Machiavellian guide to gaining, maintaining, and defending against power, distilling 3,000 years of history into 48 ruthless, strategic, and often manipulative rules. It emphasizes controlling perception, hiding intentions, building reputation, and using, rather than trusting, others.

Percy Jackson and The Lightning Thief

Written by American author Rick Riordan and published in 2005. It is a fast-paced young adult fantasy novel about Percy Jackson, a 12-year-old boy who discovers he is a demigod—the son of Poseidon. Accused of stealing Zeus's master lightning bolt, Percy embarks on a quest across the United States with his friends to find the true thief, prevent a war among the Greek gods, and uncover his own identity.
